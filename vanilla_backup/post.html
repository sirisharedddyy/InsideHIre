Aced My Google SDE Interview: A Detailed Guide and My 3-Month Prep Strategy

Alex Chen Follow
Computer Science · Class of 2026 · Dec 15

The journey to landing an offer from Google was filled with rigorous preparation, countless LeetCode problems, and a deep dive into system design principles. Today, I want to share the exact roadmap that led me to success, breaking down each round of the interview process.

1. The Phone Screening

The first step was a 45-minute technical phone interview. The interviewer focused on a medium-level algorithmic question involving graphs and BFS (Breadth-First Search). The key here wasn't just solving the problem, but effectively communicating my thought process.

"Always talk through your approach before writing a single line of code. They are evaluating your problem-solving skills, not just your syntax."

2. The Onsite Loop

The onsite loop consisted of four 45-minute interviews: three technical and one behavioral (Googlyness). The technical questions ranged from dynamic programming to challenging tree traversals. I found that recognizing patterns in the problems was crucial. For example, knowing when to apply a sliding window technique saved me valuable time.

3. Behavioral & Googlyness

Don't underestimate the behavioral round! Be prepared to discuss past conflicts, times you failed, and how you handled ambiguous situations. Use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to structure your answers clearly.

If you're currently preparing, stay consistent. It's a marathon, not a sprint. Good luck to everyone out there grinding!
